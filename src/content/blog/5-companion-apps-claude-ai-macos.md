Claude is already powerful on its own. But the right companion apps turn a good AI session into a great one.

These five macOS apps each solve a different friction point in the Claude AI workflow. They are all free or low-cost, they all work without getting in the way, and together they cover the full arc of a Claude session: getting in, staying in, staying comfortable, and getting value out.

---

## 1. Raycast — Summon Claude from Anywhere

**Free tier available. [raycast.com](https://www.raycast.com)**

The biggest tax on your Claude workflow is switching contexts. You are deep in a document, you need to ask Claude something, so you open a new tab, wait for the page to load, find the input box, type your question. By the time you get back to your document, you have lost the thread.

Raycast solves this. It is a free macOS launcher that lets you invoke Claude with a single keyboard shortcut from anywhere on your Mac, without switching apps or opening a browser. Ask your question, get the answer, return to work. The whole interaction can take under ten seconds.

The Claude AI extension for Raycast is free and connects to your Claude account. Power users also use Raycast for clipboard history, window management, and dozens of other shortcuts, so there is a good chance you will find yourself using it well beyond Claude sessions.

**Best for:** Users who context-switch frequently, developers, anyone who finds browser tabs disruptive.

---

## 2. Superwhisper — Talk to Claude Instead of Type

**Free tier available. [superwhisper.com](https://superwhisper.com)**

Typing long, precise prompts is one of the quiet frustrations of working with Claude. You know what you want to say, but the act of writing it out slows the thinking down, and the gap between your verbal fluency and your typing speed means your prompts are often shorter and less detailed than they could be.

Superwhisper is a macOS voice transcription app that converts speech to text with high accuracy and pastes it wherever your cursor is. You speak your prompt, it appears in Claude's input box, and you send it. The result is that your prompts become longer, more natural, and often better, because you are no longer rationing words to save typing time.

For users with ADHD, dyslexia, or anyone who thinks faster than they type, this is one of the highest-impact tools in the list.

**Best for:** ADHD users, heavy Claude users, anyone who thinks better when speaking than typing.

## 3. Goodmood — Make Your Screen Comfortable for Long Sessions

**Free. [colorlenz.com](https://colorlenz.com)**

Claude sessions are cognitively demanding. You are reading long responses, constructing careful prompts, thinking hard. That mental load is real, and it compounds when your screen is adding to it.

Most AI interfaces, including Claude, use bright white backgrounds and high contrast text. That is fine for a ten-minute interaction. For a two-hour working session, it is a slow drain on your visual system that eventually shows up as fatigue, loss of concentration, and that end-of-day feeling of having stared too hard at a screen.

Goodmood is a free macOS screen filter that floats gently on top of your entire display. It does not change how Claude works. It does not interfere with any interface, block any button, or capture any data. It simply places a soft color overlay over your screen to reduce brightness and visual noise.

The [Coffee Shop Series presets](/presets) were designed specifically for AI work sessions:

- **Green Tea Latte** softens the screen with a matcha tone, ideal for reading-heavy Claude sessions
- **Caramel Latte** adds warmth for writing and creative collaboration

Both are available free at the [Goodmood Preset Library](/presets). Copy the code, open Goodmood, paste in Settings.

**Best for:** Anyone doing sustained Claude sessions, users with ADHD or sensory sensitivity, evening workers.

## 4. Obsidian — Build a Knowledge Base from Claude's Best Outputs

**Free. [obsidian.md](https://obsidian.md)**

Claude produces a lot of valuable output. Explanations, frameworks, summaries, code, ideas. Most of it disappears into a chat history that is difficult to search and easy to lose.

Obsidian is a free local-first note-taking app built around linked notes. When Claude produces something worth keeping, you paste it into Obsidian, give it a title, and link it to related notes. Over time you build a personal knowledge base that grows with your Claude usage, and that you actually own since everything lives as plain text files on your machine.

Many Claude power users develop a rhythm: use Claude to think and explore, use Obsidian to store and connect. The two tools have different strengths and together they cover both.

For a practical starting point: create one Obsidian note per project you work on with Claude. Paste in the most useful responses. Add your own notes on what worked and what did not. After a month you will have something genuinely valuable.

**Best for:** Knowledge workers, researchers, writers, anyone who uses Claude repeatedly on the same topics.

## 5. Lungo — Keep Your Mac Awake During Deep Sessions

**Free. [sindresorhus.com/lungo](https://sindresorhus.com/lungo)**

This one is almost embarrassingly simple, but it solves a real problem.

When you are deep in a Claude session, reading a long response or thinking through a problem, your Mac goes to sleep. The screen locks. You break focus to wake it up and type your password. The thread is gone.

Lungo is a tiny free menu bar app by Sindre Sorhus that prevents your Mac from sleeping for as long as you want. One click, your Mac stays awake. Click again, normal sleep behavior returns. That is the entire app.

No subscription, no setup, no permissions beyond the basics. It is the kind of tool you install once and forget about, and then notice immediately when you are on a machine that does not have it.

**Best for:** Everyone. This is a universal quality-of-life improvement for any focused work session.

## The Full Setup

If you want to run all five together:

1. **Raycast** handles access — Claude anywhere, instantly
2. **Superwhisper** handles input — speak your prompts instead of typing
3. **Goodmood** handles comfort — gentle screen filter for the long session
4. **Obsidian** handles output — capture and connect what Claude produces
5. **Lungo** handles continuity — your Mac stays awake for the whole thing

Each one is free or has a meaningful free tier. None of them require an account linked to your Claude usage. None of them interfere with how Claude works.

They just make the experience of using Claude better, in five different ways, for different moments in the same session.
