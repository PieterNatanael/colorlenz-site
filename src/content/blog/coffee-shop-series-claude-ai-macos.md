## The Overlooked Variable in Your AI Workflow

When you sit down to work with Claude AI on your Mac, you're doing something cognitively intense. You're reading long, dense responses. You're constructing precise prompts. You're thinking deeply, revising, comparing, and synthesizing.

And you're doing all of that while staring at a bright white interface on a screen that's probably set to full brightness.

The screen itself becomes a problem. Not because Claude's design is bad, it's actually quite clean. But because the human eye wasn't built to stare at a backlit rectangle for hours at a time, especially not while performing sustained cognitive work.

Visual fatigue builds silently. By hour two of a deep Claude session, many users notice their thinking feels slower, their concentration shallower. They assume they're just tired. Often, the screen is doing it to them.

---

## What Goodmood Does and What It Doesn't

Goodmood is a lightweight macOS screen filter that floats gently on top of your entire display. It works with any app: Claude AI in the browser, the Claude desktop app, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Notion, your code editor, everything.

What it does:

- Places a soft color overlay over your screen to reduce brightness, contrast, and visual noise
- Runs as a floating transparent layer. Nothing is captured, recorded, or transmitted
- Works in the background with zero setup, no account required

What it doesn't do:

- It doesn't change how Claude AI works
- It doesn't interfere with your interface, buttons, or text selection
- It doesn't block, modify, or slow down any app

You get 100% of Claude. Every response, every feature, every keyboard shortcut. Goodmood just changes how the light reaching your eyes feels, like putting on a pair of tinted glasses where the tint is tuned precisely for screen work.

## Introducing the Coffee Shop Series

The Coffee Shop Series is a set of Goodmood preset codes designed for focused, sustained computer sessions. The kind of work you do when you open Claude and don't plan to close it for a while.

The idea came from a simple observation: the best cognitive environments tend to share something in common. A quiet café. A warm reading nook. A study at dusk. These spaces feel *right* for deep thinking because they reduce visual noise without reducing visibility. You can still read, still see, still work, but the environment isn't fighting your attention.

We built two presets to bring that quality to your screen.

---

### Green Tea Latte

**Best for:** Long reading sessions, working through complex Claude responses, focused research, code review.

Green Tea Latte applies a soft matcha-toned monochrome overlay with a gentle inverted filter. The result is a screen that feels quieter and less visually aggressive while keeping text crisp and readable.

If your Claude sessions involve reading and absorbing long responses, this is the preset for that. The matcha tone reduces the blue-white brightness that tires the eyes fastest, replacing it with a muted green that reads as calm to the visual cortex.

**Vibe:** Calm. Focused. Unhurried.

---

### Caramel Latte

**Best for:** Writing with Claude, creative collaboration, long prompt sessions, evening work.

Caramel Latte uses a warm sepia filter, the visual equivalent of turning the overhead fluorescents off and switching to a lamp. If you've ever noticed that your best thinking happens in warmer light, this is why: warm color temperatures signal to your brain that the environment is safe and settled, reducing the low-level alertness response that bright screens trigger.

For users who work with Claude on writing, brainstorming, or any task that benefits from a more reflective mental state, Caramel Latte creates exactly that environment.

**Vibe:** Warm. Thoughtful. Creative.

## How to Apply a Preset

Getting either preset onto your screen takes under thirty seconds:

1. Visit the [Goodmood Preset Library](/presets) and click **Copy code** on the preset you want
