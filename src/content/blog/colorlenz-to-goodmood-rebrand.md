We built a Mac screen filter and called it ColorLenz. It made sense at the time — it was a lens for your color, a filter for your screen. Functional name. Accurate enough.

But after a while, we noticed something. The people who loved the app were not talking about color. They were talking about how they felt. Calmer. Less overwhelmed. Able to get through a long afternoon without their eyes aching. A student told us they could finally read for two hours straight. Someone with ADHD said their screen stopped feeling like it was shouting at them.

None of that is about color. That is about mood. That is about feeling okay while you work.

The name had to change.

---

## Why ColorLenz Did Not Fit Anymore

ColorLenz described the mechanism — adjust the color, like a lens. But it missed the point of what we were actually building.

We were not building a color tool. We were building a screen companion. Something that sits alongside you all day, quietly making things a little easier. A thing that gets out of the way so you can do your work without your screen working against you.

When you describe a product by what it does technically, you attract people looking for a technical tool. When you describe what it feels like to use, you find the people who actually need it.

We needed a name that said: this is not about precision. This is about feeling good at your screen, from morning to night.

---

## Why Goodmood

Goodmood is direct. It says exactly what we are trying to help you have.

It is also warm without being clinical. It does not promise to fix anything. It does not claim to be medical or scientific. It just says: we made this so you feel good. That is the whole thing.

And there is something to the word "mood" that fits the product specifically. Your screen does affect your mood. The harshness of white, the edge of too much contrast, the relentlessness of a bright display in a dim room — these things add up over a day. Goodmood is about taking some of that edge off. Not dramatically. Quietly. In a way you mostly feel rather than notice.

---

## What Changed

The app is now called **Goodmood: Screen Companion** on the Mac App Store.

The app itself is the same — same filter, same presets, same privacy-first approach. Nothing about how it works has changed. If you were using ColorLenz before, your settings carry over. The presets you saved are still yours.

The website at colorlenz.com is still here while the domain transition is underway. We are moving over gradually, the way any transition that matters should go — carefully, without breaking things.

---

## What Stays the Same

Everything that made ColorLenz worth using:

- Free to download, with the core filter included
- No data collected, no screen content ever leaving your device
- A quiet overlay that works on top of any app
- Presets you can build, save, and switch between instantly
- The [Preset Library](/presets) with free codes from the community

The name changed. The reason we built it did not.

---

## One More Thing

We added the Companion — an optional feature that sits alongside the filter and shows gentle, time-aware messages and occasional eye-care reminders. Things like a prompt to look away for twenty seconds, or a quiet good morning when you first sit down. It is off by default. It does not observe what you do.

We built it because the feedback kept saying the same thing: people did not just want a screen filter. They wanted something that felt like it was on their side.

That is what Goodmood is trying to be.
